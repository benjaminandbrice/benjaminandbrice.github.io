Accommodations

We found many really good accommodations options near the venue, in the village of Montagne and in the medieval town of Saint-Émilion! Here’s a compilation of our top recommendations:

  • Camping on the venue
  • Yelloh! Village
  • Our guest houses
  • Other guest houses in Montagne
  • In Saint-Émilion

These are just suggestions of course, and the list is not exhaustive, so feel free to do your own research!

Important Note! The week-end of our wedding will coincide with the Journées du patrimoine (European heritage days), which are a big event in France especially in heritage sites like Saint-Émilion. This means that the town of Saint-Émilion will be very busy with visitors that week-end. Hotels, guest rooms, and other accommodations in town and nearby are going to be booked very quickly. In fact, a number of hotels and guest houses that we’ve called are already fully booked. For this reason we recommend that you book an accommodation as soon as possible. Thankfully we’re giving you many options, especially with Yelloh! Village and Our guest houses.

Here’s a map we made, containing all the places mentioned listed on this page: (or open in Google Maps)

Camping on the venue

Distance: 0
Cost: 0

Château Saint-Georges lets us set camp in the garden Saturday night, the best option if you’re looking to save some money! But you’ll have to bring your own tent and equipment (Brice’s family can maybe lend some), and bear in mind that you won’t have access to a bathroom with showers (only toilets).

Yelloh! Village

Distance: 15 min walk / 3 min drive
Cost: 25-55€/night* per bedroom**
*Price for a minimum of 2 nights (they charge the same total for 1 or 2 nights).
**Almost all cabins have 2 or 3 bedrooms, so they would be shared by groups of 2-6 people.

This is a nice family-type holiday camping “village” that is super close to the venue!

They have many “cottages” for rent, which are small cabins with a living room, kitchen, 1 or 2 bathrooms, and 1 to 3 bedrooms (with 1 double bed or 2 single beds per bedroom). Rental of one cottage ranges from 120€ to 220€ for 1 or 2 nights. Depending on the cottage, bed sheets and cleaning are either included or extra. They also rent pitches for setting up a tent (or parking an RV), for around 20€/night.

The village also offers many activities for no extra charge: a water park, a lake where you can go canoeing and fishing, tennis and volleyball courts, ping pong tables, a soccer field, and more. They also have many amenities on site (grocery store, bar, etc).

Make sure you check out Yelloh! Village’s website for more info and details.

Reservations are now open. Feel free to book your own cottage, in which case you can mention you’re with “Benjamin & Brice wedding” to be located near other wedding guests. We’re also happy to help or make the reservation for you. Please let us know if you’re interested in sharing a cottage with other guests, so that we can help team you up!

Our guest houses

We have booked several entire guest houses, anticipating that many of you will be interested in having a room in them. Five of them are in Montagne, so very close to the venue. The last one is in the center of the beautiful city of Saint-Émilion, a 5 minute drive away. Details are below. Let us know as soon as possible if you’d like to request a room in one of these guest houses! Please note that you may only rent a room for two nights (from Friday night to Sunday morning).

  • Le Gîte Ambrois (Montagne)
  • Gîte la Mirandelle (Montagne)
  • La Belle Ambroise (Montagne)
  • Château Croix-Beauséjour (Montagne)
  • L’Estampe (Montagne)
  • Les Logis de la Tourelle (Saint-Émilion)

Le Gîte Ambrois

Distance: 10-15 min walk / 3 min drive
Cost: 85€/night* for a bedroom
*Only available for 2 nights

“Le Gîte Ambrois” (or Saby Guesthouse) is a beautiful guest house, and it is super close to the venue. The house consists of 8 bedrooms, 8 bathrooms, 3 kitchens, a living room, a dining room, a large terrace, and more. Each bedroom has its own bathroom, a double bed, and a couple rooms have an additional single bed. Huge glass panels offer an incredible view on the rolling vineyards. The owner of the house is a vigneron and proposes extras such as visits of the vineyards, wine tasting and catered meals. Here is their website with more photos: click here.

Gîte la Mirandelle

Distance: 30 min walk / 5 min drive
Cost: 85€/night* for a bedroom
*Only available for 2 nights

This beautiful guest house in Montagne has five bedrooms, each with its own bathroom. There’s also a kitchen, a living room, and more. And there’s a swimming pool in the yard! Check out their website for more photos: Click here. Note: checkout at 12pm on Sunday.

La Belle Ambroise

Distance: 10-15 min walk / 3 min drive
Cost: 85€/night* for a bedroom
*Only available for 2 nights

“La Belle Ambroise” is another really beautiful guesthouse, and it’s actually the immediate neighbor of “Le Gîte Ambrois”! So it’s also super close to the venue. It has 3 bedrooms and 2 bathrooms.

Château Croix-Beauséjour

Distance: 20 min walk / 3 min drive
Cost: 65€/night* for a bedroom including breakfast
*Only available for 2 nights

This small wine château that has 3 nice bedrooms, for only 65€/night per bedroom including breakfast.

L’Estampe

Distance: 20 min walk / 3 min drive
Cost: 100-140€/night* for the whole house (for 2-6 people)
*Only available for 2 nights

This small and beautiful guest house is in Montagne, right by Château Croix-Beauséjour. The house has 3 levels: on the ground level there’s a kitchen and a living room with a convertible sofa, on the second floor there’s a bedroom with a double bed, a bathroom and a restroom, and on the third floor there’s a room with 2 twin beds. So it can accommodate up to 6 people, and the price is 100€/night for 2 people + 10€/night per extra person, e.g. 140€/night for 6 people. Check out their website for more photos: Click here.

Les Logis de la Tourelle

Distance: 50 min walk / 10 min drive
Cost: 100€/night* for a bedroom or 400€/night* for the whole house, includes breakfast
*Only available for 2 nights

This charming guest house is located in the beautiful medieval town of Saint-Émilion. Breakfast is included. It has four bedrooms, each with its separate bathroom. One of the bedrooms is actually a small “apartment” with a small living room and an extra sofa bed. There’s also a couple extra single beds in the other bedrooms; the whole house can fit 12 people if rented by a single group. Check out this website for more photos: Click here.

Other guest houses in Montagne

Here are other guest houses in the village of Montagne, so very close to the venue:

Les Marronniers: This guesthouse is attached to a restaurant (but separate). It contains 4 bedrooms (3 with double beds, one with bunk beds). The price is around 490€ for two nights for the whole house, plus 160€ per extra night. Check out their website for more info and photos.

Au Charmant des Vignes: This guesthouse has 2 bedrooms which they rent separately,  for 75-90€/night. Check out this website for more info and photos.

Une nuit à Montagne Saint-Émilion: An authentic and affordable guest house in Montagne. It is a bit far from the venue: 50 min walk, 7 min drive. Rooms cost about 70€/night including breakfast. Check it out on booking.com: Click here.

Castel Saint-Joseph: A fancy-looking guest house in Montagne, very close to the venue. There isn’t too much info on the internet, but it’s referenced on Google Maps: click here, and there’s photos on this website: click here.

Clos de la Barbanne: This high-end guesthouse has 1 bedroom and 3 suites to rent by the night. Prices are 180-210€/night (incl. breakfast). Note that this one is further from the venue: 40 min walk, 10 min drive. Check out their annoying website for more info and photos.

Airbnb: There is a couple of other options on Airbnb and the other usual booking websites: Google Maps (search for hotels), tripadvisor, chambres-hotes.fr, booking.com, etc. So feel free to do your own research!

In Saint-Émilion

Maybe you’d rather stay in the beautiful medieval town of Saint-Émilion? It’s a bit too far to walk (around 50 min), but a very short drive (6 min). There’s a couple dozen hotels and guest houses in Saint-Émilion, some of them very high-end, with prices ranging 80-250+€/night for a bedroom. You should hurry and make a reservation if you want to stay in Saint-Émilion, because rooms are expensive and most places will be fully booked as the date approaches.
